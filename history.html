Currimao is a coastal town located in the province of Ilocos Norte, Philippines. Its history is intertwined with the broader history of the region and the Philippines. Here's a brief overview of the history of Currimao, Ilocos Norte:

Pre-Spanish Era: Before the arrival of the Spanish colonizers in the late 16th century, the area that is now Currimao was inhabited by indigenous peoples, primarily belonging to the Ilocano ethnic group. These early settlers engaged in farming, fishing, and trade with neighboring communities.

Spanish Colonization: The Spanish colonizers established their presence in Currimao, just as they did in many parts of the Philippines. The Spanish influence is still evident in the town's culture, traditions, and architecture. The Church of Saint William the Hermit, a historical landmark in Currimao, is a testament to the enduring impact of Spanish colonization on the region.

Agriculture and Fishing: Throughout its history, the primary economic activities in Currimao have been agriculture and fishing. The town's proximity to the South China Sea has made it an ideal location for fishing, which remains a vital industry for the community.

World War II: Currimao, like many other places in the Philippines, witnessed the effects of World War II. The war left its mark on the region and its people, with several historical accounts of wartime events in the area. Modern Development: In recent years, Currimao has experienced modernization and development. Improved infrastructure, tourism, and local industries have contributed to its growth. The town is known for its beautiful beaches, attracting tourists seeking a tranquil escape by the sea.

Cultural Heritage: The Ilocano culture is deeply ingrained in Currimao's identity. The town celebrates various cultural festivals and traditions, often featuring traditional music, dance, and cuisine. Today, Currimao remains a place of historical significance, natural beauty, and cultural richness. It's a testament to the enduring spirit of its people and their ability to adapt to changing times while preserving their rich cultural heritage.
